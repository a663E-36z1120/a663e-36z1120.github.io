 About My Name

My full legal name is Andrew Hanzhuo (汉卓) Zhang (张). I consider both "Andrew" and "Hanzhuo" to be my first name, separated by a space and in that order. However, please just call me "Andrew" since the full thing is such a mouthful!

The "Zh-" in the Chinese parts of my name are pronouced like the "Dr-" in "Draw" or "Drive". There are many ways to interpret these three Chinese characters, my favorite one is as follows:

Despite its chauvinistic connotations in modern Mandarin, the character "汉" (Hàn) is composed of the radical "氵" that denotes relation to water, and the character "又" that means repetition or "again" (Note: this is only true in simplified Chinese, which is the variant that I use; in traditional Chinese, "汉" is written "漢"). "氵" and "又" together can mean "an expansive body of water". This is actually closer to how the character was originally used in ancient texts to denote specific expanses of water or even the miky way, which was at one point imagined to be a massive river in the sky.

On the other hand, the character "卓" (Zhuó) simply means outstanding or eminent. Together, "汉卓" can denote an eminent, great expanse of water; to me specifically, this is Lake Ontario, in a city by which I was brought to this world, and along the shores of which I dedicated a considerable portion of my free time.

My family name "张" (Zhāng, trad. "張") is one of the most common family names in China and in the Chinese diaspora, and it is really interesting if we look at its composition. The character "弓" on the left means bow and the character "长" (trad. "長") on the right means long, extended (both time and space), or "to grow". Together, "张" is a bow that has been extended and drawn, which is why some legends say those of us with the "张" family name are the descendents of ancient bowmen and archers! When used as a verb, "张" also means to open in an intentional way. To me, my family name is a reminder for me to always be ready as a drawn bow, and more importantly to openly embrace what comes next.

I believe that our relationship with AI does not have to be adversarial, and if bridged properly and effectively, the two worlds could become the same continent where AI could be a catalyst for our next step in evolution! (Think symbiogenesis of the eukaryotic cell from prokaryotic organisms!) As a result, I have geared my education towards understanding artifical intelligence systems better and working with analytical software and hardware systems with the assistance of AI that helps to understand our bio-organic intelligence better.
